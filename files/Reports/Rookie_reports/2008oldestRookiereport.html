Steve keeps insisting that I'm a 20+ yr. rookie. Personally I think he is full of stuff and
don't consider myself anywhere near a rookie status. But I like to have fun and will play
along. So I had one battle that I actually fought in. It was Thursday. I told Robert I
wanted to battle at least one battle at NATS and he agreed. So I set off in the first sortie.
Went straight into the battle and received quite a few hits. On a brief inspection after the
first sortie I had 12 belows and about 20 just above the waterline. The MARYLAND was in
no danger of sinking but knowing from experience with this much damage she would not
survive long in the next sortie if I went into battle. I came up with a great plan. I would
put the MD in the water between the piers so she wouldn't drift off and accidentally sink
in deep water. I would leave her there and then go over to where everybody else was
standing/ preparing to battle. After waiting oh about 10 or 15 minutes battle was
declared and I called 5. About a minute passed and somebody says where is the
MARYLAND? Then a couple captains look at me as if I'm going to tell them where I am.
The Axis were not too concern with the MD after all, their Admiral gave orders to look for
other game. Well Robert was concerned that the MD would sink. Can't say I blame him he
doesn't know what it takes to sink the MD. So he goes over toward where the MD is
sitting. Adrian and Brandon both watched Robert head over to the pier. Axis Captains do
have common sense and can real quickly put 2 and 2 together. So I see 2 class 4 ships
head off from the battle and towards the pier. Not much I can do as this point being my
wonderful plan went down the tubes. I would of hope I turned my pump off at this point
but I really don't remember. I walked on over to the pier and there sits the MD. Just as I
left her. Floating perfectly between the piers. Brandon and Adrian must thought it was
Christmas with a beautiful broadside of the MD just sitting there. Brandon was so
shocked that he asked what he should do. "Well! Brandon shoot”. And shoot he did. She
gracefully sank in 6 inches of water. After I had pulled her out and put her on the table I
walked down to the lake and Brandon was getting ready to come off 5. OFF 5? Seams
Brandon unloaded his stern guns into the MD and didn't have anything left to shoot.
It was a great battle. True my plan got totally screwed by MY SON. It would of been
great if it worked. It would not of made a difference in the battle whether or not I sunk.
Pulling the wool over the eyes of the Axis was the objection and I failed. Win lose or draw
having fun is better. The MD has sunk too many times to count. Most I don't remember
this one I will. Thanks Adrian, Brandon and Robert for the fond memory of my only
combat mission at NATS 08.
