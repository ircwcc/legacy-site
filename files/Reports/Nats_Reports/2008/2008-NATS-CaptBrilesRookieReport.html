Capt. Briles Rookie Report
NATS 2008

Before I can even begin to write about the concept that is IRCWCC
Nationals I have to give credit where credit is due. Every rookie has
that person or in my cases persons who help, push, persuade,
sabotage, and generally keep the rookie in question interested in the
hobby. I would like to thank everyone at the Hayes Ship Yards for
their generous help and knowledge, Foremost Mr. Marty Hayes, Mr.
Steve Andrews, Matt Andrews, Mr. Tommy, as well as all the others.
With that said on to NATS.

Saturday, As we arrived at Lake Conron I set up my bunk and helped
with the setup for the weeks activities. The entire time I listened
to the vets talk about past NATS and hilarious stories about even
funnier people. Eventually the day came to close after watching a
couple of war movies to set the mood for the week.

Sunday, Everyone showed up for breakfast and a little after 10 the
boats were put on the water after being tested for legality. My SMS
Scharnhorst sunk during Speed trials, A start to the week that put a
few of my fellow Captains in laughter and nervous looks.

Monday, The proving ground. During the first fleet battle I handled
myself relatively well managing not to sink myself or ram anyone, a
tricky thing when one is captaining a Scharnhorst. The second Fleet
battle went just as well with me only bumping into my much larger
playmate Rob Arena in his Alabama. Rob decided to put me through my
paces and chased my Scharnhorst around the lake. After out turning
him and getting a few good shots off he left me alone.

Tuesday, what a day there was the spectacular sink of the Boston that
resulted in Matt Andrews and myself diving around trying to find it.
Luckily Mr. Don Fisher found it in his cool scuba gear. There was
also first one on one between me and  Monkey. He and I traded
shots and unfortunately I rammed him as he pulled a devious Allied
Stall tactic. All in all though the battle was very good and a lot of
fun to be in.

Wednesday, I proved how deadly the Scharnhorst was in one on ones.
I had a rematch with Monkey. This time I switched to a more
defensive set of tactics. As the Boston can not turn with a
Scharnhorst I let him try to set up on me then turned so as he
crossed either my bow or stern while revealing his water line. This
battle was one possibly my favorite of all NATS.

Thursday was a typical day with during fleet battle. Nothing
spectacular happened. Convoy was in the afternoon. This was a hectic
hour, I pulled my boat off the water in order to better help the axis
fleet and in particular Mrs. Gingers/ Platos Shinano. It was a blast
and there were many beautiful sinks including Robs Saratoga? And
Matts Von Der Tann.

Friday was mainly clean up and then the awards dinner. It was really
a lot of fun, I have to say that I was blown away when at the dinner
I got the rookie of the year award, all of the rookies at NATS though
few of us were really all awesome. And this is my story of NATS 2008
