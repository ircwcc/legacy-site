Location of Battle:

Greenbrier State Park
21843 National Highway (US Route 40)
Boonsboro, MD  21713-9535

Entrance fee at the gate: $4 for in-state, $5 for out-of-state

After leaving the entrance, drive to the bottom of the hill (at the "T") , turn right and follow the road to the boat landing. To the right of the ramp follow the dirt road around to the battling area to unload. You can drive on the grass to unload but must move the cars back to the maintenance lot as soon as possible.

Combatants:

NOTE: Length, weight and CO2 regulator pressure checks will be done at the hotel on Sunday instead of at the lake as usual. Speed and waterline checks will occur starting PROMPTLY at 7am on Monday, June 23.

In the IRC, the 30 sec. moss rule is always in effect. Speed or other rules violations can be reported to the Contest Director or your admiral. If there is something on your ship that is legal under another set of rules but not in the IRC, check with the Contest Director for a ruling. The cost of drinks, food, CO2 is covered by your entrance fee and will be collected at the contest. Any visitors who do not run a ship will pay only for food and drinks if they partake.

Visitors:

The battle area is marked with yellow tape, anyone within the taped off area MUST wear safety glasses. There is a box of safety glasses for visitors at the entrance to the area, please return them there when finished with them. (they cost money!)

The pit area is open for visitors as long as safety glasses are in place, and the combatants love explaining the game, and their ships to you. However, in between the sorties, they may be too busy reloading or repairing for the next battle to converse.

During the battle, stay up the hill away from the battlers who are watching their ships and might walk right over you if you get in their way. Yes, the ships do shoot bb's and these can hurt if you are hit. However, with the exception of the eyes, teeth and ears, damage will not be great. Watch young children to make sure they do not take off their safety glasses or sit down.
